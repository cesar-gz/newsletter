Israel to pause fighting for four hours daily in northern Gaza, U.S. says[1]: According to the White House National Security Council spokesperson, Israel will implement four-hour pauses in fighting in parts of the northern Gaza Strip each day. This is to allow humanitarian aid and civilians to enter and exit the area. The spokesperson said the U.S. is working with Israel and other partners to facilitate the delivery of assistance to Gaza.

Turkey's Erdogan, on Israel-Hamas war, says West is 'too weak to even call for a cease-fire'[2]: Turkey’s president, Recep Tayyip Erdogan, criticized the West on Thursday for its response to the Israel-Hamas war, saying it’s “too weak to even call for a cease-fire.” He accused some Western countries of being “partners in bloodshed” and said Turkey will continue to support the Palestinian cause.

US Senate Democrats tee up bill to avert government shutdown[3]: U.S. Senate Majority Leader Chuck Schumer on Thursday took a procedural step to allow the Democratic-majority chamber to pass a stopgap government funding bill before a Nov. 17 deadline to avert a shutdown. The bill would fund the government through Feb. 18, 2024, and also raise the debt ceiling to avoid a default. The bill faces opposition from Republicans, who want Democrats to use a separate process to address the debt limit.
